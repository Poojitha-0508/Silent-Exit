## Finding 1 — Recency is the #1 Churn Driver

| Recency Bucket | Churn Rate |
|----------------|------------|
| 0–30 days | 13.88% |
| 31–90 days | 13.75% |
| 91–180 days | 22.24% |
| 181–365 days | 38.13% |
| 365+ days | **52.31%** |

**Insight:** Customers inactive for 365+ days churn at 52.31% — nearly 4x higher than recent buyers at 13.88%.

**Recommendation:**
- Trigger automated re-engagement email/SMS at **90 days of inactivity**
- Offer personalized discount at **180 days** to win them back
- Flag **365+ day** inactive customers as at-risk and escalate to retention team

---

## Finding 2 — Membership Tier Directly Controls Churn

| Tier | Churn Rate |
|------|------------|
| None | 48.41% |
| Silver | 34.65% |
| Gold | 26.24% |
| Platinum | 20.37% |

**Insight:** None-tier customers churn at 48.41% — more than double the Platinum rate of 20.37%. Every tier upgrade significantly reduces churn.

**Recommendation:**
- Offer free Silver trial after 2nd purchase for None-tier customers
- Show None-tier customers what benefits they are missing
- Target high-spend None-tier customers for Gold upgrade offers

---

## Finding 3 — Churn is Growing Month-Over-Month

| Period | Monthly Churned |
|--------|----------------|
| Jan 2021 | 1 |
| Mid 2022 | ~400 |
| Early 2023 | ~1,000 |
| Mid 2024 | **3,500+** |

**Insight:** Monthly churn grew from just 1 customer in Jan 2021 to 3,500+ in May 2024 — a 3,500x increase indicating an escalating retention crisis.

**Recommendation:**
- Set up monthly churn rate alert — trigger if churn exceeds set threshold
- Investigate product or pricing changes that caused the 2023 acceleration
- Implement proactive retention strategy before churn compounds further

## Finding 5 — Demographics Do NOT Drive Churn

| Dimension | Churn Rate Range | Conclusion |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Device | 32.87% – 33.87% | No impact |
| Age Group | 32.91% – 34.87% | No impact |
| Gender | 36.76% – 37.62% | No impact |
| Channel | 36% – 38% | No impact |

**Insight:** Churn is nearly identical across all devices, age groups, genders, and acquisition channels (~33-38%). Demographics do NOT predict churn.

**Recommendation:**
- Do not build demographic-specific retention strategies — waste of budget
- All retention spend should focus on **recency-based re-engagement**
- Focus on WHEN customers buy, not WHO they are or HOW they buy
